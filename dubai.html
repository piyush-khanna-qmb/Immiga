Dubai Immigration

Your comprehensive guide to immigration opportunities, visa requirements, and settlement options in Dubai, UAE.

Immigration Services for Dubai

Employment Visa

Dubai’s employment visa allows foreign nationals to work for a company based in Dubai. The process involves obtaining a job offer, medical testing, and submitting the relevant documents. The visa is typically sponsored by the employer.

Investor Visa

For individuals looking to invest or start a business in Dubai, the Investor Visa allows residency in exchange for investing in or owning a company. This type of visa is ideal for entrepreneurs and business owners.

Remote Work Visa

Dubai offers a special visa for remote workers, allowing foreign nationals to live in the city while continuing their work remotely. This is ideal for freelancers and digital nomads looking for a new location to work and live.

Family Visa

This visa allows expatriates working in Dubai to sponsor their family members for residency. Eligible family members may include spouses, children, and parents.

Student Visa

Dubai offers student visas for international students enrolled in academic institutions. Students can stay in the city for the duration of their studies and may also work part-time during their education.

Golden Visa

The Golden Visa program provides long-term residency to investors, entrepreneurs, skilled workers, and exceptional talents. This visa offers a wide range of benefits, including a 5- or 10-year residency period without the need for a sponsor.

Required Documents

Personal Documents

  • Valid passport
  • Recent passport-sized photos
  • Birth certificate
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
  • Police clearance certificate from country of origin

Employment Documents

  • Employment contract or job offer letter
  • Previous employer reference letters
  • Educational qualifications
  • Proof of work experience

Financial Documents

  • Bank statements
  • Proof of sufficient funds for support
  • Investment documents (for investor visa)
  • Business financial records (for entrepreneur visa)

Note: Document requirements may vary depending on the specific visa type and individual circumstances. All documents not in English or Arabic must be translated into either language.
