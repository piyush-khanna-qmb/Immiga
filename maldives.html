Maldives Immigration

Your comprehensive guide to immigration options, visa requirements, and residency opportunities in the Maldives.

The Maldives is a tropical paradise known for its stunning islands, luxurious resorts, and vibrant marine life. With its growing economy and status as a global tourism and investment hub, the Maldives is an attractive destination for expatriates, investors, and professionals from around the world.

The Maldives offers various immigration pathways for foreigners, from work and business permits to residency options for investors and retirees. The country's immigration policies are designed to encourage foreign investment, tourism, and skillful professionals to contribute to the nation's growth.

At ImmigaTech, we provide expert services to guide you through the Maldives immigration process, whether you are planning to work, start a business, or retire in this beautiful archipelago.

Immigration Services for Maldives

Employment Visa

The Employment Visa is granted to foreign nationals employed by Maldivian companies. The employer acts as a sponsor, submitting the necessary documents and paying visa fees for the employee. This visa is ideal for professionals seeking work in the Maldives.

Investor Visa

The Maldives offers an Investor Visa for individuals who wish to invest in the country's economy. Investors must meet specific financial requirements and demonstrate their ability to contribute to sectors such as tourism, real estate, or hospitality.

Retirement Visa

The Maldives provides residency opportunities for retirees from abroad. The Retirement Visa allows individuals over a certain age to settle in the Maldives and enjoy a peaceful lifestyle in one of the world's most serene locations.

Family Visa

The Family Visa allows expatriates to bring their immediate family members (spouse and children) to live with them in the Maldives. It is typically tied to the primary visa holder’s employment or investment status.

Student Visa

Students wishing to pursue education in the Maldives can apply for a Student Visa. The visa is issued to international students enrolled in a recognized educational institution and is valid for the duration of the course.

Tourism & Hospitality Work Permit

The Maldives’ thriving tourism industry offers work permits for foreign nationals employed in hotels, resorts, and other tourism-related businesses. These work permits can be issued for both short-term and long-term employment opportunities.

Immigration Process

  • Eligibility Assessment

    We assess your background, qualifications, and goals to determine the best immigration pathway for you in the Maldives. Whether for employment, business, or retirement, we help you identify the best option for your situation.

  • Visa Strategy Development

    Once we’ve assessed your eligibility, we create a tailored strategy to guide you through the visa application process. We’ll identify the necessary documents and determine the best approach to ensure a successful application.

  • Document Collection

    Our team will assist in collecting the required documentation for your visa application. This may include personal identification, financial statements, employment contracts, or investment certificates, depending on your visa type.

  • Application Submission

    We ensure that your visa application is complete and accurately submitted to the relevant authorities. We handle the entire process, including filling out forms and paying required fees.

  • Medical Examination

    Some visa categories may require a medical examination to ensure applicants meet health standards. We assist in scheduling and preparing for medical tests through authorized clinics in the Maldives.

  • Visa Approval and Issuance

    Once your application is approved, you will receive your visa. We will guide you through the steps necessary to activate your visa and prepare for your arrival in the Maldives.

  • Post-Arrival Support

    Our services continue after your arrival in the Maldives. We assist with settling in, obtaining local identification, and integrating into the community, making sure your transition is smooth and hassle-free.

Required Documents

Personal Documents

  • Valid passport
  • Recent passport-sized photographs
  • Birth certificate
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
  • Police clearance certificate

Employment Documents

  • Employment offer letter or contract
  • Proof of previous work experience
  • Educational qualifications

Financial Documents

  • Bank statements and financial records
  • Proof of investment (if applying for Investor Visa)
  • Retirement fund documentation (for Retirement Visa)

Note: Document requirements may vary depending on the visa type and individual circumstances. All documents not in English or Dhivehi must be translated into one of these languages.
