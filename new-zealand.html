New Zealand Immigration

Your complete guide to immigration pathways, visa requirements, and residency options in New Zealand.

New Zealand is one of the most sought-after immigration destinations, known for its breathtaking natural beauty, high quality of life, and a welcoming multicultural society. With a strong economy and a focus on attracting skilled workers, students, and entrepreneurs, New Zealand offers a range of immigration pathways to suit diverse needs.

The New Zealand immigration system is designed to fill skill shortages, reunite families, and encourage investment. Whether you're seeking to work, study, or start a business, New Zealand provides various visa options to help you achieve your goal of permanent residency.

At ImmigaTech, we specialize in helping you navigate New Zealand’s immigration process, providing expert advice and personalized guidance throughout your journey to the Land of the Long White Cloud.

Immigration Services for New Zealand

Skilled Migrant Visa

The Skilled Migrant Visa is for individuals who possess the skills and qualifications needed in New Zealand’s labor market. Applicants are assessed based on a points system that considers factors such as age, work experience, and qualifications.

Work to Residence Visa

This visa allows skilled workers to live and work in New Zealand for up to 30 months, with the potential to apply for permanent residency after meeting certain requirements.

Student Visa

New Zealand offers Student Visas to international students enrolled in accredited educational institutions. A Student Visa allows individuals to work part-time during their studies and provides pathways to post-graduation work visas and residency.

Partner Visa

This visa is designed for individuals who are in a genuine relationship with a New Zealand citizen or resident. It allows the partner to live, work, and study in New Zealand, with the potential for permanent residency.

Investor Visa

The Investor Visa is available to individuals who wish to invest in New Zealand’s economy. There are different categories depending on the amount of investment, ranging from NZD 1.5 million to NZD 10 million.

Entrepreneur Visa

The Entrepreneur Visa allows individuals to establish or buy a business in New Zealand. Successful entrepreneurs may apply for permanent residency after running their business for a specified period and meeting other criteria.

Immigration Process

  • Eligibility Assessment

    We begin by assessing your eligibility for various New Zealand immigration pathways. We consider factors such as age, work experience, qualifications, and health to determine the best visa option for you.

  • Visa Strategy Development

    Based on the results of the eligibility assessment, we develop a personalized immigration strategy to guide you through the visa application process, ensuring it aligns with your long-term goals in New Zealand.

  • Language Proficiency

    Most visa categories require proof of English language proficiency. We help you prepare for recognized English language tests such as IELTS or TOEFL to meet the requirements for your application.

  • Document Preparation

    We assist with collecting, organizing, and submitting the necessary documents required for your visa application. This includes personal identification, educational qualifications, work experience, and financial statements.

  • Application Submission

    Once all documents are ready, we submit your visa application to the appropriate New Zealand authorities. We ensure that all forms are completed accurately and meet the latest requirements.

  • Health and Character Checks

    As part of the visa process, you may need to undergo health checks and submit police clearance certificates. We guide you through the medical examination process and ensure you meet the health and character requirements.

  • Visa Approval and Notification

    After your application is processed, you will be notified of the decision. If approved, we guide you through receiving your visa and preparing for your arrival in New Zealand.

  • Post-Landing Support

    We offer post-landing support to help you settle into New Zealand. This includes assistance with obtaining local identification, setting up accommodation, finding employment, and integrating into New Zealand society.

Required Documents

Personal Documents

  • Valid passport
  • Birth certificate
  • Recent passport-sized photographs
  • Police clearance certificate

Employment Documents

  • Job offer letter or employment contract
  • Detailed resume or CV
  • Evidence of work experience (reference letters)

Educational Documents

  • Degree certificates and diplomas
  • Transcripts of academic records
  • English language proficiency test results (IELTS, TOEFL)

Financial Documents

  • Bank statements and proof of funds
  • Investment records (for Investor Visa)
  • Tax returns or financial statements

Note: Document requirements may vary depending on the visa category and individual circumstances. All documents must be in English or accompanied by certified translations.
